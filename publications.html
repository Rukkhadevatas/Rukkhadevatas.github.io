Thesis

  1. Asymptotic Betti Bounds for Complete Intersection, the supervisor is Dingxin Zhang, SIMIS.
  2. Introduction: In a recent result, Xuanyu Pan, Dingxin Zhang, and Xiping Zhang established an asymptotic upper bound for the Betti numbers of a hypersurface Y of degree d inside an arbitrary singular variety X, uniformly with respect to all such Y ⊂ X. In this project, we extend their technique to the case where Y is a complete intersection in X. For simplicity of the argument, we assume that X itself is a complete intersection in \mathbb{P}^n. This result can be viewed as a generalization of earlier work of Daqing Wan and Dingxin Zhang, which treats only the case X=\mathbb{P}^n. For more general varieties X, the argument can be generalized after replacing the perverse degeneration lemma used here with the proper degeneration lemma proved in PZZ's work. The motivation for obtaining upper bounds on Betti numbers comes from arithmetic applications related to the Weil conjectures. Our proof strategy relies on the computation of characteristic classes together with techniques from the theory of perverse sheaves. First, we compute the Euler characteristic of a general Y using characteristic class methods. We then decompose Y into an affine open subvariety and a lower-dimensional closed subvariety. Using the excision long exact sequence, we obtain a bound for the Euler characteristic of the affine part. Next, applying Nicholas M. Katz's Euler characteristic trick, we derive bounds for the Betti numbers in the affine case. We then apply a perverse degeneration lemma to degenerate the estimate to the affine part of the special fibre, as the perversity condition yields weak Lefschetz theorem that controls the middle homology. Finally, we recover the result for the entire projective variety Y by induction on the dimension, noting that the complement of the affine part is a lower-dimensional subvariety.
